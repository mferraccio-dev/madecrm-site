The problem

Most CRMs were built foreveryone — and no one.

Bloated tools you don't use

Salesforce-class platforms force a $200/seat workflow on a five-person team. You pay for AI, marketing automation, forecast modeling — and use 8% of it.

Spreadsheets that don't scale

The shared Sheet works until two people edit it, until you need pipeline reporting, until the pasted-in contact data starts diverging from reality.

Half the team isn't logging anyway

Even when you have a CRM, data goes stale because nobody has time to update it. Managers lose visibility. Deals slip — not because anyone dropped the ball, but because nobody could see it coming.
